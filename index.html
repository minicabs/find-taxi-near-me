Frequently Asked Questions

1. How do I know if a taxi is taken?

Taxis typically have a roof sign that shows whether they’re available. When the taxi is unoccupied, the light on the sign is illuminated, indicating it's ready for service.

3.How does booking a taxi work?

Then, add your destination, select the type of taxi service you prefer, and choose your payment method. You can also leave a note for the driver if you like. Finally, tap “Schedule” or “I’m Ready Now” and you're all set!

4. Is it better to book a taxi in advance?

Yes, booking a taxi in advance is often the better choice—especially during peak hours, airport transfers, or important appointments. It guarantees availability, allows you to choose your preferred vehicle, and ensures the driver arrives on time. With Minicabs.co.uk, you can pre-book 24/7 for peace of mind and fixed pricing.

5. Can taxi drivers ask for money upfront?

In most cases, licensed taxi or minicab drivers in London should not ask for full payment upfront unless it’s a pre-booked journey with a fixed fare. However, some operators may request a deposit or upfront payment for long-distance or late-night trips. Always confirm the fare in advance, especially when booking through apps or private hire service
